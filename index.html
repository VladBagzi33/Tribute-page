Emperor Dusan the Mighty

Emperor Dušan the Mighty is one of the most important Serbian medieval rulers.

Here's a time line of Dusan the Mighty life:

  • 1308.-It is assumed that he was born around 1308, but according to the historical data of his biographers, 1311 and 1312 are also mentioned. His father was the famous Serbian king Stefan Dečanski, and his mother was the Bulgarian princess Teodora Smilec.
  • 1322.-However, his father, Stefan Dečanski, ascended the throne. In the same year, Dušan will be crowned as the young king, after which he will be given the administration of the Zeta region.
  • 1330.-Battle of Velbužde. The conflict that took place between the army led by King Stefan Dečanski and the army of the Bulgarian emperor ended with a Serbian victory. Namely, the king's troops, in which the Madi king Dusan stood out, completely defeated the army of the Bulgarian emperor.
  • 1331.-The conflict between King Dušan and his father Stefan Decanski. He crowned himself king and officially took the throne.
  • 1333.-He married the sister of Bulgarian Emperor Ivan Alexander, Jelena.
  • 1346.-In Skopje, the Serbian and Bulgarian patriarchs crowned Stefan Dušan as the emperor of the Serbs and Greeks.
  • 1349.-In Skopje, he brings a code, better known as "Dušan's code".
  • 1350.-Conflict with Jovan Kantakuzina.
  • 1355.-Establishes peace on the Serbian-Hungarian border.
  • 1355.-He passed away on Sunday, December 20. He was buried in his endowment at the Monastery of the Holy Archangels near Prizren.

"There are several legends. According to one, Emperor Dušan was never canonized for organizing the brutal murder of his father, King Dečanski. In other legends, it is claimed that before the outbreak of the plague, he fled to Hilandar with his wife Jelena.
